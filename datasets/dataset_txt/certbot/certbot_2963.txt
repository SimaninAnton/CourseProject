When using --csr and --cert-path, the certificate is placed in a different location than what was specified by the user.
root@connor:~# /usr/local/bin/openssl req -new -newkey rsa:2048 -keyout dank.ninja.key -nodes -subj "/CN=dank.ninja" -reqexts SAN -config <( cat /usr/local/etc/ssl/openssl.cnf; echo "[SAN]"; echo subjectAltName=DNS:dank.ninja,DNS:www.dank.ninja ) -outform der -out dank.ninja.csr
Generating a 2048 bit RSA private key
.......................+++
....................................................+++
writing new private key to 'dank.ninja'
-----
root@connor:~# letsencrypt certonly --csr dank.ninja.csr --cert-path dank.ninja.crt

IMPORTANT NOTES:
 - Congratulations! Your certificate and chain have been saved at
   /root/0001_chain.pem. Your cert will expire on 2016-02-12. To
   obtain a new version of the certificate in the future, simply run
   Let's Encrypt again.
